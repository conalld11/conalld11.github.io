“Biotech Breakthroughs”: Genetics Experiment

Our experiment began with DNA extraction. This is the process of rubbing swabs inside your mouth, scraping off the inner cheek cells, in order to obtain a DNA sample. There was no eating or drinking before this step, to keep the sample pure. The next step of the experiment was PCR. PCR is the process of amplifying a large number of copies of a specific segment of DNA for closer study. Using this step, we created billions of copies of our own DNA so that we ourselves could examine our own DNA better. After PCR, we had to measure the length of our DNA, using a process called gel electrophoresis. Gel electrophoresis is the process of using an electric current to push DNA fragments through a gel, separating them by size so their length can be measured. We then took a photo of the finished results, and then used Photoshop to measure the length of the DNA bands; plugging those numbers into a Google spreadsheet with all the data from everyone in the class, to determine DNA matches. In the end, the individuals I matched with were all part of very different ethnic groups than I, myself. In the end, I learned many things about DNA and the fascinating process of how it is extracted, tested, and measured, but one fact rises above all else I learned: DNA is not biologically real. Throughout the entire experiment, my team and I documented all the steps of the process through various pictures and videos, called B-roll, including two interviews, one before the experiment and one after. My team and I edited these clips together to make a documentary of the experiment. I was personally tasked with editing together the clips of the interview that was conducted after the experiment. Overall, the whole process was a wonderful experience and a dynamic opportunity for me to extend my knowledge of DNA.

The process of editing and creating a documentary demanded mastery in my creativity and innovation, and I was up to the challenge. I tried my absolute hardest to make an original, dynamic, and clean video using every drop of creativity and innovation in my body; it was worth it when I was able to be proud of my final result.
