My Year in New Media and Technologies for Social Change Class!

In my freshman year of high school, I took a class called "New Media and Technologies for Social Change", and it was one of—if not the most—diverse and unique courses I have taken in my life. It was incredibly different than all the other classes I have taken, and I enjoyed it very much. In this class, I had the privilege to work on many dynamic projects, but today I will only talk about two: my “Our Shared Canvas”: Screenprinting Project and my “Biotech Breakthroughs”: Genetics Experiment.

“Our Shared Canvas”: Screenprinting Project

The ¨Our Shared Canvas¨: Screen printing project was my first real exposure to real art. Never before had I actually cared so much or worked as hard as I did on my screen printing. In the screen printing art project, I aimed to raise awareness about global warming through a unique artwork featuring a personified image of the Earth. I, inspired by famous screen printing works, conceptualized a design with their eyes and the text "Our Fault" to convey a message about environmental degradation. After capturing a photo and using Adobe Photoshop to isolate my eyes, I created a background of Earth with generative AI. The project involved designing and building a wooden frame using Adobe Illustrator, measuring and cutting wood, assembling the frame, and attaching a mesh screen. Despite challenges with the screen's tightness, the artist proceeded to print their artwork using vinyl and a handheld heat press, resulting in a final product printed on various materials. Throughout the process, I recorded various clips and videos for B-roll. At the end, I made a short film documenting this project using Adobe Premiere. In the end, the whole experience was an amazing, eye-opening experience that I feel really connected me with the arts.

“Biotech Breakthroughs”: Genetics Experiment

Our experiment began with DNA extraction. This is the process of rubbing swabs inside your mouth, scraping off the inner cheek cells, in order to obtain a DNA sample. There was no eating or drinking before this step, to keep the sample pure. The next step of the experiment was PCR. PCR is the process of amplifying a large number of copies of a specific segment of DNA for closer study. Using this step, we created billions of copies of our own DNA so that we ourselves could examine our own DNA better. After PCR, we had to measure the length of our DNA, using a process called gel electrophoresis. Gel electrophoresis is the process of using an electric current to push DNA fragments through a gel, separating them by size so their length can be measured. We then took a photo of the finished results, and then used Photoshop to measure the length of the DNA bands; plugging those numbers into a Google spreadsheet with all the data from everyone in the class, to determine DNA matches. In the end, the individuals I matched with were all part of very different ethnic groups than I, myself. In the end, I learned many things about DNA and the fascinating process of how it is extracted, tested, and measured, but one fact rises above all else I learned: race is not biologically real. Throughout the entire experiment, my team and I documented all the steps of the process through various pictures and videos, called B-roll, including two interviews, one before the experiment and one after. My team and I edited these clips together to make a documentary of the experiment. I was personally tasked with editing together the clips of the interview that was conducted after the experiment. Overall, the whole process was a wonderful experience and a dynamic opportunity for me to extend my knowledge of DNA.

The process of editing and creating a documentary demanded mastery in my creativity and innovation, and I was up to the challenge. I tried my absolute hardest to make an original, dynamic, and clean video using every drop of creativity and innovation in my body; it was worth it when I was able to be proud of my final result. The use of this 21st-century skill enhanced my learning process and led to a final result that far surpassed my expectations.
